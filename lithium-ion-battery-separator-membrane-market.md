<p><p>The lithium-ion battery separator membrane market can be categorized into two primary types based on manufacturing processes: wet and dry. The wet process involves dissolving polymers in a solvent, followed by casting and drying, resulting in high porosity and superior performance. Conversely, the dry process uses less solvent, enhancing environmental sustainability, and often features simpler manufacturing steps. Both types are crucial for ensuring effective ionic conductivity and preventing short circuits in lithium-ion batteries, driving advancements in energy storage technology.</p></p>
