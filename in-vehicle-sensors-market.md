<p><p>The in-vehicle sensors market is divided into two primary types: wired and wireless sensors. Wired sensors are connected directly to the vehicle's electrical system, offering reliable data transmission and minimal latency but requiring complex installation. In contrast, wireless sensors use radio frequencies or Bluetooth, providing flexibility and easier installation but may face interference or require battery management. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing vehicle safety, performance, and connectivity, catering to the evolving demands of modern automotive technology.</p></p>
