<p><p>The molecular spectroscopy instruments market comprises various types, each with unique applications. Near-Infrared Spectroscopy (NIRS) is primarily used for agricultural and food analysis, measuring moisture and composition. Fourier-Transform Infrared Spectroscopy (FTIR) provides detailed molecular fingerprints, essential in chemical and pharmaceutical industries. Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR) facilitates structural identification of compounds, widely used in research labs. Ultraviolet-Visible Spectroscopy (UV-Vis) analyzes electronic transitions in molecules, often applied in chemistry and biochemistry for concentration and purity assessments.</p></p>
