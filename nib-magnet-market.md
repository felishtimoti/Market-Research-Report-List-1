<p><p>The NIB magnet market can be categorized into two primary types: bonding and sintering. Bonding involves the use of adhesives to combine magnet particles, resulting in flexible and lightweight magnets suitable for various applications, including small electronics. Sintering, on the other hand, is a process that involves high-temperature treatment of powdered materials to form dense, rigid magnets with superior magnetic properties, often utilized in automotive and industrial sectors. Both types cater to different performance and application requirements.</p></p>
