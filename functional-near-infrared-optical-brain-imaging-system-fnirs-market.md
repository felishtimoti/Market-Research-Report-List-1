<p><p>Functional Near Infrared Optical Brain Imaging System (fNIRS) is a non-invasive neuroimaging technology that measures brain activity by detecting changes in blood oxygenation and hemodynamics in response to neuronal activity. By utilizing near-infrared light, fNIRS can penetrate the skull and monitor cerebral blood flow, providing real-time insights into brain function. This technology has seen increased adoption in clinical research, cognitive neuroscience, and rehabilitation settings due to its portability, cost-effectiveness, and ease of use compared to other imaging modalities like fMRI and PET.</p><p>The fNIRS market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.1% during the forecast period, driven by rising investments in brain research, an increasing prevalence of neurological disorders, and growing interest in brain-computer interface technologies. Emerging trends include the integration of fNIRS with other neuroimaging techniques and advancements in sensor technology, enabling higher resolution and accuracy. Additionally, the expansion of applications in fields such as psychology, education, and sports science supports market growth. The ongoing development of wearable fNIRS systems is also anticipated to enhance accessibility and user engagement, further propelling the market forward.</p></p>
