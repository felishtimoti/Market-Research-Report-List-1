<p><p>The copper recycling market consists of two primary segments: pure copper and copper alloy. Pure copper is highly sought after for its excellent conductivity and is often derived from scrap wires and electrical components. In contrast, copper alloys, which include metals like brass and bronze, contain varying amounts of copper mixed with other elements, making them suitable for specific applications in construction, electronics, and automotive sectors. Both segments contribute significantly to sustainability by reducing the need for virgin copper mining.</p></p>
